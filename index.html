The Growing Waste Crisis

  • India generates 62 million tons of waste annually, with wet waste (organic kitchen waste) comprising nearly 50% of it.
  • Improper disposal of wet waste leads to landfills, methane emissions, and groundwater contamination, severely affecting public health and the environment.
  • Most urban societies lack proper waste segregation systems, leading to mixed waste that cannot be recycled or composted efficiently.

Challenges with Existing Waste Management Systems

  • Lack of proper waste segregation: Wet waste gets mixed with plastic & non-biodegradable waste, making recycling & composting ineffective.
  • Overloaded landfills: Landfills release toxic gases (methane, CO‚ÇÇ), contributing to global warming & pollution.
  • Unhygienic waste disposal: Open dumping attracts pests, causes bad odors & spreads diseases.
  • High transportation costs: Municipalities spend huge amounts on collecting & processing unsegregated waste.
  • Lack of composting infrastructure: Organic waste, which could be composted, is wasted in landfills.
